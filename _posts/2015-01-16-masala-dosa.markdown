I will start with another disclaimer. The characters, incidents, places and things are real (except the fact that I added some masala into it!). If you think that it has any resemblance to you, your places, or your thingies, it's better not to tell anybody. Also, make sure that you don't come face to face with the person on the homepage of this blog!!

**1. Everybody says "You are innocent"**

I am not a huge fan of the item mentioned in the title. But I like them. There are two main reasons for that:

* In Kerala, we don't make masala dosas often at home. We usually have it when we go out to some restaurants.
* Even if we make it at home, it is different. Better actually, but we still like to have it from restaurants.

Innocent children don't wish for anything more than a masala dosa. So I started having masala dosas. Eventually, I created a small mental list of restaurants where we get good ones. Believe me, they were really good.

**2. Everybody says "After joining college, you have to be professionals and matured"**

Time went on...

Every story has a flip side. Here, it started when I finished my school and joined a college. Maturity is when we stop having masala dosa and shift to noodles and fried rice. It's not always the case, but sometimes it is true.

That was the time when somebody started a food court near our college. We all were so happy that we got a new place to have food. I went there with my friends. Just out of curiosity, we decided to try masala dosa from there.

"It must be good, because it is costly"...

Sigh!

Unfortunately, that was the worst one I'd tried ever in my life. I even thought that I can make better masala dosa than that (given I have zero knowledge about cooking). It was a (not-so-)good experience. We stopped haing masala dosa and shifted to noodles and fried rice.

**3. Everybody says "After getting a job, you have to be serious about life"**

Time went on...

I completed my Engineering and got a job in Bangalore. Seriousness comes when we shift back to healthy but not costly food from noodles and fried rice (Being serious about life, we have to save money).

After coming to Bangalore, going to restaurants became a daily routine. Being an admirer of masala dosa, I wanted to try that first in Bangalore.

Hmmm...

Sigh, again!

It turned out to be a disaster. Masala Dosa here doesn't taste like in Kerala. There were two main reasons for that:

* Dosa here is very hard. Ask my Mom, she knows that I don't like hard ones.
* Sambar tastes sweet.

I didn't like both the changes. But just to make sure, I tried it at different places. But the result was the same.

And I stopped having masala dosas from Bangalore. I got interested in Burgers and Pizzas also. And thus, I lost my chance to be serious about life...

Over the course of time, I started having food from another restaurant. I usually have idli from there because of two main reasons:

* I don't get to eat idli often in Bangalore.
* My experience with Bangalore masala dosa was not so nice

One day, villain came to me in the form of 'snooze'. I had no other option, but to have masala dosa. I woke up late and idli was over by the time I reached there.

But, unexpectedly villain turned out to be a good guy!!

The two main reasons that made me hate Bangalore masala dosa weren't there. It was more like the one we used to get in Kerala. 

And then, I started having masala dosas again.

PS: I am yet to be serious about life!
